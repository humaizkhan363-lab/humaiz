7 Wonders of Pakistan

Explore the most iconic landmarks that define Pakistan’s history and culture.

Discover the Wonders

Mohenjo-Daro

An ancient Indus Valley civilization site dating back to 2500 BCE, showcasing early urban planning.

Rohtas Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site built by Sher Shah Suri near Jhelum, known for its grand defensive walls.

Badshahi Mosque

A Mughal-era masterpiece in Lahore, symbolizing the grandeur and artistic brilliance of Mughal architecture.

Minar-e-Pakistan

Located in Lahore, it marks the site where the Pakistan Resolution was passed in 1940.

Karakoram Highway

Also called the 8th wonder of the world, it connects Pakistan and China through breathtaking mountain ranges.

Shah Jahan Mosque

Built in Thatta during Mughal rule, it features intricate tile work and perfect acoustic design.

Faisal Mosque

Located in Islamabad, it’s Pakistan’s largest mosque, known for its unique desert tent-inspired design.
